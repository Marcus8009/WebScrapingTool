Thanks for sharing Jared - While I appreciate the evolving nature of AI interactions, likening them to human colleagues oversimplifies a complex relationship. 

The more fundamental question for me is: How do humans relate to machine intelligence? I don't think we have an answer to this...yet. 

Treating AI as a colleague can lead to anthropomorphization, causing people to believe an LLM truly understands them. AI outputs are often persuasively framed with definitive and prescriptive qualities, making it easy to accept without questioning for hallucinations or credibility of sources. 

Much of the AI tools we have today lack the explainability a human colleague brings. When we collaborate with humans, we understand their thought processes, biases, and limitations. AI, however, remains largely a "black box" - they're easy to use but difficult to understand. 

We need to consider the reliance and trust we place in AI, it's different from collaborating with a colleague. 
I'd argue this is new territory - not calculator or colleague, but perhaps somewhere along that spectrum.
